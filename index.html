Theory

Tortuosity of a path is a measure of how deviant the path is from a straight line.

In the context of porous materials, tortuosity corresponds to the cumulative deviation of the fluid acoustic velocity from the bulk fluid motion given by the following expression: \[ \alpha_{\infty} = \frac{\displaystyle{\frac{1}{V}\int_{V}v^{2}dV}} {\left( \displaystyle{\frac{1}{V}\int_{V}\vec{v}dV} \right)^{2}} \]

If the path travelled is a straight line, the tortuosity is 1.

Higher the tortuosity, less straight the path is.
